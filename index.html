Medical Image Analysis using Deep Learning

January 2025
Mumbai, MH, India

Robust Training Procedures in Deep Learning Models for Histopathology Image Analysis

Motivated by the need to assist pathologists in faster and more reliable diagnosis—especially in breast cancer, which affects over 2.3 million women globally each year—I focused my Master’s research on developing robust learning methods for medical image analysis.
Designed robustness-focused training strategies to handle label noise, OOD samples, and weak supervision using sample re-weighting and optimized loss functions.
I implemented an AAE-based model leveraging latent-space likelihoods for dynamic sample weighting (Binary, Normalized, Hybrid) on BreakHis and BACH datasets, demonstrating improved robustness over standard CNNs.
I gained 12.5% (BreakHis) and 6.18% (Endometrium) accuracy with stable AUC under 20% noise using semi-supervised MixMatch enhanced with energy shaping, k-medoids clustering, and contrastive pretraining—enabling reliable breast cancer classification under limited labeled data.
